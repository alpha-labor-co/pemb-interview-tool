Interviewer script
Read this to the applicant
Word for word or in your own words — your call.
What to say
Open with
"Hey, do you have about five minutes? We like to do a quick skill evaluation with everyone we talk to — it's nothing to stress over, just a few questions so we can get a feel for your background."
Who we are
"Alpha Labor Co is a specialized erection staffing company. We put PEMB crews on active projects across the country — steel, roofing, wall panels, insulation. What we do differently is we don't just throw bodies at a job. We take the time to figure out what you're actually good at and match you to the projects where you're going to perform the best."
Why the evaluation
"The reason we do this evaluation is simple — we want you to succeed on the job, not just show up. If we know your strongest area is roofing or primary steel or wall panels, we can put you where you're going to thrive, move up faster, and make more money. That's the whole point."
Our culture — this matters
"One thing I want you to know about Alpha Labor Co — this company was founded by people who came up doing this work. We're not the company that says the customer is always right and leaves it at that. We find the middle ground. We care about our employees just as much as we care about our customers. At the end of the day, we want everybody to win. When the customer wins, we win. When you win, we all win. We want to see you grow, make more money, and build a career — not just fill a slot on a project."
Transition into the evaluation
"Alright, I'm going to ask you a handful of questions about your experience and knowledge. A couple things before we start — some of these questions might sound really basic, maybe even obvious. That's intentional. We ask them because the answers tell us a lot about where someone's at, so don't overthink it. And if you genuinely don't know the answer to something, just say so. 'I don't know' is always the right answer when it's the honest one. We're not here to trick you — we just want to understand what you know and what you're good at so we can put you in the right place. Sound good? Let's go."
Interviewer note
Keep it conversational — you don't have to read this word for word. The goal is to make them comfortable before the questions start. Emphasize that "I don't know" is always acceptable. A relaxed applicant who answers honestly is far more useful than one who guesses to try to impress you.
